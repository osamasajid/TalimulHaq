Iqaama is SUNNAT for FARZ Salaah only. It is not Sunnah for any other Salaah.
Q. If anyone performs his Farz Salaat at home, should he say Azan and Iqaama?
A. Azan and Iqaama in the neighboring Masjid will be sufficient, but it is better to say them at home also.
Q. Should a traveler say Azan and Iqaama during a journey?
A. Yes, both Azan and Iqaama should be said when one is at a lonely place. It won't matter if one says only Iqaama
and not Azan, However, it is Makrooh to leave the both.
Q. Is it permissible if one person says the Azan and another says the Iqaama?
A. If one who said the Azan is not present or if he is there but does not mind, then another person may say Iqaama.
If the person who said Azan minds, then it is Makrooh for another person to say the Iqaama.
Q. How much time should be allowed to pass between Azan and Iqaama?
A.
In all Salaat except Maghreb, one should wait for the Iqaama till the persons who are eating or
are in the toilet can join the Salaah. In Maghreb Salaat it is advisable that after the Azan,
Iqaama should be said after the lapse of the time equal to the reciting of THREE AAYATS.
