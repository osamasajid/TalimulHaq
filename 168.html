Phonology
The Arabic and English phonological systems vary extensively, not only in the range of sounds
used, but also in the relative importance of vowels and consonants in expressing meaning. While
English has 22 vowels and diphthongs to 24 consonants, Arabic has only eight vowels and
diphthongs to 32 consonants.
